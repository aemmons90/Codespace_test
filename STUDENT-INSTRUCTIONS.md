# Getting Started with GitHub Codespaces

Welcome! This activity uses **GitHub Codespaces**.

A codespace is an online workspace that opens in your browser. You do not need to install everything on your own computer first.

## Open the training repository
Go to:

https://github.com/aemmons90/Codespace_test/tree/main

## Create your own codespace
1. Click the green **Code** button.
2. Click the **Codespaces** tab.
3. Click **Create codespace on main**.
4. Wait for it to open.

That’s it — your workspace will open in the browser.

## Important: your work is separate
Each person gets their **own** codespace.

That means:
- you will not change the instructor’s workspace
- you will not change another student’s workspace
- you can safely explore and edit files

Your changes stay in **your own copy** unless you choose to save them back to GitHub.

## Do I need to fork the repository?
No.  
You do **not** need to fork anything just to get started.

## Where will I find the files?
Once your codespace opens, look at the file list on the left side.

You may see folders like:
- `modules/` — lesson materials
- `data/` — datasets
- `.devcontainer/` — setup files for the codespace

Your lesson files may end in `.qmd`. These are Quarto files.

## What should I click first?
After the codespace opens:
1. Look at the left sidebar for the files
2. Open the file your instructor tells you to use
3. Follow the instructions in that file

## What if I make a mistake?
That is okay.

Because your codespace is your own copy:
- you can experiment
- you can edit files
- you will not ruin the class repository

If something gets really messy, you can delete your codespace and make a new one.

## Do my changes affect the original repository?
No, not automatically.

Your edits only affect your own codespace unless you:
- commit changes
- push changes to GitHub

Most beginners do **not** need to do that unless the instructor asks.

## What if the codespace does not open?
Try these steps:
1. refresh the page
2. wait a little longer
3. try creating the codespace again
4. ask the instructor for help

## Quick summary
- Click **Code**
- Click **Codespaces**
- Click **Create codespace on main**
- Open the file you are told to use
- Your work is private to your own codespace

You are ready to begin.
